Welcome to Serene Quit

Your peaceful companion on the journey to a smoke-free life

Quitting smoking is one of the best decisions you can make for your health. This app will guide you through 10 essential tips to help you succeed.

1

Set a Quit Date

Choose a specific day to quit and mentally prepare yourself. Mark it on your calendar and treat it as a fresh start.

Try this:

Pick a date within the next 2 weeks - not too soon to prepare, but not too far to lose motivation.

2

Identify Your Triggers

Recognize situations that make you crave cigarettes (e.g., stress, coffee, alcohol) and plan how to avoid or cope with them.

Common triggers:

  • Drinking alcohol or coffee
  • After meals
  • Work breaks
  • Social situations
  • Stressful moments
3

Nicotine Replacement Therapy

Consider patches, gum, lozenges, or prescription medications (like Chantix or Zyban) to ease withdrawal symptoms.

Over-the-counter:

  • Nicotine gum
  • Patches
  • Lozenges

Prescription:

  • Chantix (varenicline)
  • Zyban (bupropion)
  • Nicotine inhaler

Note:

Consult your doctor before starting any medication, especially if you have health conditions.

4

Behavioral Alternatives

Replace smoking with healthier habits like chewing gum, drinking water, deep breathing, or exercise.

Drink Water

Deep Breathing

Short Walk

Chew Gum

5

Seek Support

Tell friends and family about your goal, or join a support group (online or in-person). Apps like Smoke Free or QuitGuide can also help.

Support Options:

Join a local or online support group

Download a quit-smoking app

Call a quitline (1-800-QUIT-NOW in US)

6

Avoid Smoking Environments

Stay away from places where people smoke, especially in the early stages of quitting.

Strategies:

Choose non-smoking sections in restaurants

Avoid bars or parties where smoking is common

Take different breaks at work if colleagues smoke

Clean your car and home to remove smoke smells

7

Manage Stress

Practice relaxation techniques like meditation, yoga, or exercise to reduce stress (a common relapse trigger).

Quick Stress Relief:

Try the 4-7-8 breathing technique: Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 7 seconds, exhale for 8 seconds. Repeat 3-4 times.

8

Celebrate milestones (1 day, 1 week, 1 month) with treats like a nice meal, a movie, or something you enjoy.
